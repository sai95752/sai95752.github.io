About Us.

MedComm is a service created and maintained by medical students and faculty at UCSF. It was created in March of 2020—as COVID-19 cases were rising and hospitals were getting filled to the brim—with two central goals: serving as a viable way for non-emergency patients get the care they need without adding to the flood of patients at in-person hospitals, and driving down the cost of healthcare for minor issues. We accomplished our first goal thanks to our excellent healthcare and frictionless software, built by professional software engineers with the goal of improving on current video conferencing applications. In addition, as our service is purely driven by medical students seeking practice, we care for minor issues completely free of charge, successfully accomplishing our second goal.

Services.

Assistant Medical Professionals On Call

Unlike every other telecommunications service, we provide 24/7 profession medical support. Our assistant medical professionals are certified doctors and students from the local UCSF, with skills ranging from dentistry to neuroscience. We have 300 medical professionals on call 24/7. If your need as a patient is minor, you can meet one on one with our assistant medical professionals to discuss your problem.

Translators On Call

If the patient speaks a different language than the doctor, we also have experienced translators on call. Our 400 translators speak over 50 languages. Our translators have graduated from the top universities with advanced degrees in their language.

Prescription Database

Our software has a database with all the types of medications, and it’s sorted into different categories, with descriptions, so the doctor can find medications. We have also partnered with CVS and Walgreens for medications, so doctors and patients can easily send prescriptions to either pharmacies.

Note Taking Software

Our software for calls senses the voice between the doctor and the patient, and puts it in text on the note pad which is accessible to both the patient and doctor. The notepad automatically detects the important things and highlights them in the notepad, such as taking in key words and identifying the patient’s illness the prescription given.
