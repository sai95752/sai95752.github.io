About Us

MedComm is a service created and maintained by medical students and faculty at UCSF. It was created in March of 2020—as COVID-19 cases were rising and hospitals were getting filled to the brim—with two central goals: serving as a viable way for non-emergency patients get the care they need without adding to the flood of patients at in-person hospitals, and driving down the cost of healthcare for minor issues. We accomplished our first goal thanks to our excellent healthcare and frictionless software—built with the help of our school's computer science department—with the goal of improving on current video conferencing applications with features specialized for a medical environment. In addition, as our service is purely driven by medical students seeking practice, we care for minor issues completely at extremely low costs, successfully accomplishing our second goal.

Webmaster 2021

For the 2021 Technology Student Association Webmaster Event, teams were required to “Create a fictional medical telecommunications company and design a website”. The website should serve as a guide for information about the company and their communication services provided. Present an overview of services as if you were a consumer. Provide the type of solutions your company creates, sells, and/or serves. To respond to this theme, our team created MedComm.

Design Choices

While creating MedComm, we took inspiration from other telecommunications companies, such as Zoom, Lingoace, and Skype. Jumping from aspects of user experience and attractiveness, we decided on an interactive website with a carefully designed color spectrum. We also made sure that our website was easy to navigate and was clean to the eye. Right from the homepage, we introduce the core of our company. The entire website is one consecutive page to make a slick appearance and make it easy to navigate. Each page has a title, subtitles, and groups of text to address different topics.

History

March 20th 2020 - A group of friends hop on a Zoom call and realize all of the cons, then come up with an idea for a better solution for doctors and medical patients to use. DocNet was the original idea, where we would purely have medical students treat patients online for minor purposes.

March 29th 2020 - The original draft of MedComm was finished, We built this original draft of MedComm by ourselves. We studied code diligently by ourselves on online learning platforms such as Sololearn and EDX to be able to build this platform.

April 5th 2020 - MedComm was published through the original domain MedComm.com. Originally, it was a success, however, as more and more people started using DocNet, the website crashed. We reached out to our school’s engineering department for help.

May 7th 2020 - A meeting was held between the school’s engineering department. During this meeting, the final plan for MedComm was built.

June 1st 2020 - A partnership was created between Sprint and MedComm, to provide fast and reliable connectivity for all users on MedComm.

October 21st 2020 - MedComm was completely finished, thanks to the help of our school’s engineering department. The final MedComm was published, under the domain “medcomm.us”. We reached nearly 300 million users within the first two months and our UCSF medical assistants treated over 10,000 patients with minor issues.

Services

World-Renowned Medical Consultants on Call

UCSF is renowned for being one of the best universities to study medicine in the world. Every time you get on a call using MedComm, you are talking with a prodigious student or experienced faculty member of our esteemed college. In addition, as many of our thousands of faculty members are certified doctors, we can write out prescriptions to cure minor issues at very low costs. Moreover, thanks to our university's wide variety of specializations, we offer medical expertise on more pressing issues ranging from dermatology to neuroscience, but with a higher fee.

Software-Aided Diagnosis

Our software has access to a vast database of the 10,000 most commonly prescribed medications. With the help of machine learning algorithms, as a medical consultant jots down symptoms on their virtual notepad, our software can find the best medication to treat the patient. This allows for extremely fast and accurate diagnosis.

Automatic Transcripts

Our software is equipped with a voice recognition feature that will automatically generate a transcript of everything said in the call. This makes it far easier for doctors to extrapolate the most important things a patient tells them without missing anything. This, paired with our software-aided diagnosis, leads to extremely efficient and high quality care.

Automated Translation

Unlike in-person hospitals, we can care for people from any part of the world. To fully enable this, our transcripts have a translate feature, supporting the 108 most-spoken languages in the world. Thanks to this feature, the language you speak does not determine the quality of care you receive.
